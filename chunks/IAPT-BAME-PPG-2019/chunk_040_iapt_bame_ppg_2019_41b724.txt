Importance of supervision in cross-cultural work
Good-quality supervision is an important part of ensuring the provision of high-quality therapy
(IAPT Manual, National Collaborating Centre for Mental Health 2018). The IAPT Manual is clear about the benefits of supervision in services, and there may be an increased need for this where therapists are working across cultures and trying new approaches to treatment. The core skills needed to supervise cross-cultural work are those that supervisors will have already developed and cultivated as therapists and supervisors. These skills include working collaboratively, formulating the problem clearly, using Socratic methods to encourage the therapist to think about issues and develop their own solutions, and understanding the current level of expertise on the part of the therapist, as well as the degree to which they can develop skills at that point in their development as a therapist. 25
