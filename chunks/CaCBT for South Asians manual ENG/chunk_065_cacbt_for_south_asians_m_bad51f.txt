Sleep Problems
Disturbed sleep is likely the most common (and probably the most annoying for the client) of all the symptoms of depression. It is crucial to explore the clientâ€™s ideas about sleep when a client talks about sleep disturbances. A significant proportion of people who present with sleep problems are often found to have unrealistic ideas about sleep. The amount of sleep each person needs can often vary, and the number of hours they need each night can change throughout their life. On average most people need six to seven hours of sleep. Napping in the afternoon is still very common among
South Asians and is worth exploring when discussing sleep problems. Part 3 Therapy-Related Issues II | 51
