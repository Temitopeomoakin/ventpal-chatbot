PsychologicalDistressWorkbook
When the brain gets used to feeling a certain way, it starts going there more and more, because it’s familiar and easy to get to. This is what’s called a
“negative feedback loop”.
In order to cope with their distress, most people avoid the trigger. Though avoidance helps in the immediate moment, it makes anxiety worse in the long term. Why?
Two reasons:
1) It reinforces your belief that you’re helpless to deal with it
2) you don’t get positive experiences as basis for further success
