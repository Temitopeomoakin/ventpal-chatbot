The client may find it helpful to link positive counter‐statements to ideals they value. For example, religious values may counter cognitions that devalue the individual who sees themself as a creation of God, or family‐oriented values may challenge cognitions that suggest a client’s family may not care about them. Positive self‐talk should:
• begin with an “I” (this reinforces the client’s ability to manage their situation)
• be stated in the present tense
• be credible (i.e., based on something the client really, truly believes)
