Black individuals are not solely defined by their race, but are also shaped by factors such as gender, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status, disability and more. For instance, a Black woman's experience of depression may differ significantly from that of a Black man due to the unique intersections of race and gender, as well as other factors such as socioeconomic status and access to resources. Mental health professionals must be attuned to the unique and varying presentation of these intersecting oppressions and their impact on the client’s mental health.
